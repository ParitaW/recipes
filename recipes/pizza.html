Pizza

Pizza is a beloved Italian dish that has become a global culinary phenomenon. It consists of a flattened disk of bread dough topped with some combination of olive oil, oregano, tomato, olives, mozzarella or other cheese, and many other ingredients, baked quickly—usually, in a commercial setting, using a wood-fired oven heated to a very high temperature—and served hot. Originally from Naples, Italy, pizza has evolved into countless variations worldwide, from traditional Neapolitan styles to modern interpretations. It's celebrated for its versatility, making it perfect for everything from casual dining to special occasions.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cheese
  • Pepperoni
  • Vegetables

Steps

  1. Combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water and olive oil, mix until dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic.
  4. Let the dough rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours.
  5. Punch down the dough and shape into a pizza crust.
  6. Spread tomato sauce over the crust.
  7. Top with cheese, pepperoni, and vegetables.
  8. Bake in a preheated oven until crust is golden and cheese is melted.
  9. Slice and serve hot.
